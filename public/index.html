Emerald City Resource Guide

The Emerald City Resource Guide is a directory of social services for Seattle published by the non-profit newspaper, Real Change. The guide was original published only in hard copy form with the intent to provide information to people in need who may not have access to the internet. During the first two rounds of publication, volunteers called each organization and handwrote updates to each organization's information - a time-consuming and error-prone process. This opportunity to reduce labor paired with reports from fellow organizations that the most frequent users of the guide were their own staff, I partnered with Real Change to create an online version of directory. The site is built on a Node.js stack and uses a Postgres database to manage organization data. This project is ongoing and v2 features will include username and password management so that Real Change volunteers and organizations can update their information directly.
